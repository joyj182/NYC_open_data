About NYPD


The New York City Police Department (NYPD) is the primary law enforcement agency for New York City. Established in 1845, it is the largest municipal police force in the United States, employing over 33,000 sworn officers and 15,000 civilian staff. The NYPD is responsible for maintaining public safety, enforcing laws, managing traffic, and emergency response across the city's five boroughs.
